Three Categories of Multiple Access الفئات الثلاث للوصول المتعدد
① Random Access
Contention-based

No station controls another. Any station can transmit at any time. Risk of collision.

Examples: ALOHA, CSMA, CSMA/CD, CSMA/CA
② Controlled Access
Orderly turns

Stations take turns in an organized manner. No collision. Requires coordination.

Examples: Polling, Token Passing
③ Channelization
Divided bandwidth

Bandwidth is divided so each station gets its own piece. No collision.

FDMA (freq), TDMA (time), CDMA (code)

Pure ALOHA

ALOHA الخالص
The simplest random access protocol — send whenever you want
How Pure ALOHA Works كيف يعمل ALOHA الخالص

Originally developed for satellite communication. The simplest multiple access protocol: transmit whenever you have data. If a collision occurs, wait a random time and retransmit.

3

5

Pure ALOHA Throughput Formula معادلة الإنتاجية
Pure ALOHA Throughput
S = G × e^(-2G) S = throughput (fraction of time channel carries successful frames) G = offered load (avg frames per frame-time) e = 2.718... Maximum: S_max = 1/(2e) ≈ 0.184 = 18.4% at G = 0.5
Key Insight
Even at best conditions, Pure ALOHA wastes ~81.6% of channel capacity due to collisions and idle time. It's simple but very inefficient.

Slotted ALOHA

ALOHA المجزأ بفترات زمنية
Better efficiency by synchronizing transmissions to time slots
How Slotted ALOHA Works كيف يعمل Slotted ALOHA

Time is divided into equal-length slots (each = Tframe). Stations can only begin transmitting at the start of a slot.

Key Rule: A station with data must wait for the next slot boundary. It cannot transmit in the middle of a slot.
Effect: Vulnerable time is cut in half (only 1×Tframe instead of 2×Tframe), so efficiency doubles.
Requirement
Stations must be synchronized to know when slots begin. This adds complexity compared to Pure ALOHA.

CSMA — Carrier Sense Multiple Access

CSMA — الوصول المتعدد مع الاستماع للناقل
"Listen before talk" — reduces but does not eliminate collisions
CSMA Core Idea المفهوم الأساسي
"Listen before talk" — Before transmitting, a station senses (listens to) the channel.
✅ If channel is idle → may transmit
⚠️ If channel is busy → defer based on persistence method

CSMA significantly reduces collisions compared to ALOHA because stations don't transmit into an already-busy channel. However, collisions can still occur due to propagation delay: two stations may both sense idle and start transmitting simultaneously.

Arabic
الفكرة: "استمع قبل أن تتحدث". قبل الإرسال، الجهاز يتحقق من القناة. إذا فارغة → أرسل. إذا مشغولة → تصرف حسب طريقة الـ Persistence. التصادم ممكن رغم الاستماع بسبب زمن الانتشار (Propagation delay) — قد يستمع جهازان في نفس الوقت ويجدان القناة فارغة ثم يرسلان معاً.
Three Persistence Methods طرق الاستمرار الثلاث
1-Persistent
الاستمرارية الكاملة
Busy: Keep sensing continuously
Idle: Transmit immediately (p = 1)
High collision risk
When channel goes idle, ALL waiting stations transmit at once → collision likely
Non-Persistent
غير الاستمرارية
Busy: Wait a random time, then sense again
Idle: Transmit immediately
Better efficiency
Random wait → less likely multiple stations collide; channel may sit idle unnecessarily
p-Persistent
الاستمرارية الاحتمالية
Busy: Wait for next idle slot
Idle: Transmit with probability p; defer with (1−p)
Best balance
Balance between collision risk and channel utilization
Method Channel Busy Action Channel Idle Action Trade-off
1-Persistent Keep sensing (wait for idle) Transmit immediately (p=1) High collision probability
Non-Persistent Wait random time, sense again Transmit immediately May waste channel time
p-Persistent Wait for next slot Transmit with prob. p; defer (1−p) Best balance
Arabic
ثلاث طرق للتعامل مع القناة:
• 1-Persistent: انتظر حتى القناة فارغة ثم أرسل فوراً — خطر تصادم عالٍ لأن كل الأجهزة ترسل في نفس اللحظة.
• Non-Persistent: انتظر وقتاً عشوائياً ثم استمع مجدداً — أفضل لكن القناة قد تضيع وقتاً فارغة.
• p-Persistent: إذا فارغة، أرسل باحتمال p أو أجّل باحتمال (1−p) — أفضل توازن.

CSMA/CD — Collision Detection

CSMA/CD — كشف التصادم
Used in wired Ethernet (IEEE 802.3) — detect and abort on collision
CSMA/CD Operation عمل CSMA/CD

Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Detection — the station transmits AND monitors the channel simultaneously. If it detects a collision, it immediately aborts.

1
Sense channel → if idle, start transmitting
2
While transmitting, listen to the channel
3
Collision detected? → Stop immediately, send JAM signal
4
Wait binary exponential backoff time
5
Retry from step 1 (up to 16 attempts)
Used In
Wired Ethernet — IEEE 802.3. Works because wired signals can be detected in both directions simultaneously.

CSMA/CD يرسل ويستمع في نفس الوقت. إذا اكتشف تصادماً: يتوقف فوراً، يرسل إشارة JAM لتنبيه الجميع، ينتظر وقتاً عشوائياً ثم يعيد المحاولة. يُستخدم في Ethernet السلكي لأن الأسلاك تسمح بكشف التصادم.
Minimum Frame Size in CSMA/CD الحد الأدنى لحجم الإطار
Why does minimum frame size matter?
The sender must be still transmitting when the collision signal arrives back. If the frame is too short, the sender finishes before hearing the collision → never knows it happened!
Minimum Frame Size Formula
T_transmission ≥ 2 × T_propagation Min Frame Size = 2 × T_prop × Bandwidth Where: T_prop = max propagation delay (one way) 2 × T_prop = round-trip time (worst case)
Worst Case Scenario
Station A transmits, station D (farthest away) starts transmitting just before A's signal arrives. A must be transmitting long enough to hear D's collision signal return.

CSMA/CA — Collision Avoidance

CSMA/CA — تجنب التصادم
Used in wireless LANs (IEEE 802.11 / Wi-Fi) — avoid before they happen
Why Can't Wireless Use CSMA/CD? لماذا لا يستطيع الاتصال اللاسلكي استخدام CSMA/CD؟
The Hidden Terminal Problem
Station B can hear A and C, but A cannot hear C (and vice versa). A and C might transmit simultaneously without knowing it — collision happens at B but neither A nor C can detect it.
Signal Fading
A wireless transmitter's own signal is much stronger than any incoming signal. While transmitting, the station cannot hear a collision signal — it's drowned out.
Hidden Terminal Problem
A
B
C
A can't hear C — both may transmit, collide at B
Arabic
في الشبكات اللاسلكية: (1) مشكلة الجهاز المخفي — A لا يسمع C لذا لا يعلم أنه يرسل → تصادم عند B. (2) المُرسِل لا يستطيع سماع التصادم لأن إشارته القوية تطغى على الإشارات الأخرى. لذلك نتجنب التصادم بدلاً من كشفه.
CSMA/CA — How It Works كيف يعمل CSMA/CA

CSMA/CA uses two mechanisms to avoid collisions:

① IFS — Interframe Space
A mandatory waiting gap between frames. Different priority levels use different IFS lengths — shorter IFS = higher priority access.
② Contention Window
After sensing idle + waiting IFS, the station waits a random number of slots within a window before transmitting. All competing stations pick different random numbers → less collision chance.
Contention Window Size
Window Size = 2^K - 1 K = attempt number (K=1,2,3,...) K=1: window = {0..1} → 2 slots K=2: window = {0..3} → 4 slots K=3: window = {0..7} → 8 slots (Larger K = larger window = less collision)
Counter Freezing
While counting down the random slots, if the channel goes busy, the station freezes its counter and resumes counting only after the channel becomes idle again.

CSMA/CA يتجنب التصادم بطريقتين:

② نافذة التنافس (Contention Window): اختر عدداً عشوائياً من الخانات وانتظر. إذا صارت القناة مشغولة أثناء العد → جمّد العداد حتى تفرغ القناة. حجم النافذة = 2^K − 1.

Example 1 — ALOHA Backoff Times مثال: أوقات الانتظار في ALOHA
Problem: Calculate possible values of TB when stations on an ALOHA network are a maximum of 600 km apart.
Step-by-step solution
Step 1: Calculate propagation time (one way) Tp = distance / speed Tp = (600 × 10³ m) / (3 × 10⁸ m/s) Tp = 2 ms Step 2: Backoff time formula TB = R × Tp, where R ∈ {0, 1, ..., 2^K - 1} Step 3: Apply for K=1 R ∈ {0, 1} → TB ∈ {0 × 2ms, 1 × 2ms} TB ∈ {0ms, 2ms} Step 4: Apply for K=2 R ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3} → TB ∈ {0ms, 2ms, 4ms, 6ms} Step 5: Pattern — for K=3: TB ∈ {0ms, 2ms, 4ms, 6ms, 8ms, 10ms, 12ms, 14ms}
Key takeaway
Each collision attempt doubles the possible wait times. This is Binary Exponential Backoff — same concept used in CSMA/CD Ethernet.
Arabic
أولاً: حساب Tp = 600,000 ÷ 300,000,000 = 2ms
عند K=1: يختار R من {0,1} → TB ∈ {0ms, 2ms}
عند K=2: يختار R من {0,1,2,3} → TB ∈ {0ms, 2ms, 4ms, 6ms}
كلما زاد عدد المحاولات، زادت الخيارات → أقل احتمال للتصادم مجدداً.
Example 2 — CSMA/CD Minimum Frame Size مثال: الحد الأدنى لحجم الإطار
Problem: A CSMA/CD network has a bandwidth of 10 Mbps. If the maximum propagation time is 25.6 μs, what is the minimum size of the frame?
Step-by-step solution
Given: Bandwidth (BW) = 10 Mbps = 10 × 10⁶ bps Propagation delay = 25.6 μs = 25.6 × 10⁻⁶ s Formula: Min Frame Size = 2 × T_propagation × Bandwidth Calculation: Min Frame Size = 2 × (25.6 × 10⁻⁶) × (10 × 10⁶) Min Frame Size = 2 × 25.6 × 10 Min Frame Size = 512 bits Convert to bytes: 512 bits ÷ 8 = 64 bytes Answer: Minimum frame size = 512 bits = 64 bytes
Real World
This is exactly why the minimum Ethernet frame size is 64 bytes! Frames smaller than 64 bytes are called "runts" and are discarded by Ethernet switches.
Arabic
Min Frame Size = 2 × T_prop × BW = 2 × 25.6μs × 10Mbps = 512 bits = 64 bytes
هذا هو سبب أن الحد الأدنى لحجم إطار Ethernet هو 64 بايت في الواقع!
Example 3 — ALOHA Throughput Calculation مثال: حساب الإنتاجية
Problem: A Pure ALOHA network has G = 0.5. What is the throughput? A Slotted ALOHA network has G = 1. What is its throughput?
Solution
Pure ALOHA at G = 0.5: S = G × e^(-2G) S = 0.5 × e^(-2 × 0.5) S = 0.5 × e^(-1) S = 0.5 × 0.3679 S = 0.184 = 18.4% ← MAXIMUM for Pure ALOHA Slotted ALOHA at G = 1: S = G × e^(-G) S = 1 × e^(-1) S = 1 × 0.3679 S = 0.368 = 36.8% ← MAXIMUM for Slotted ALOHA

Slotted ALOHA: S = 1 × e^(−1) = 0.368 = 36.8% (الحد الأقصى عند G=1)

Exam Questions & Answers

أسئلة الامتحان والإجابات
Expected exam questions with complete answers
Q1 — Conceptual
What is the maximum throughput of Pure ALOHA and Slotted ALOHA? Why is Slotted ALOHA more efficient?

Answer: Pure ALOHA max = 18.4% at G = 0.5 (formula: S = G·e−2G). Slotted ALOHA max = 36.8% at G = 1 (formula: S = G·e−G). Slotted ALOHA is exactly twice as efficient because it halves the vulnerable time from 2×Tframe to 1×Tframe by only allowing transmissions at slot boundaries.
Q2 — Calculation
A CSMA/CD network has a bandwidth of 10 Mbps. If the maximum propagation delay is 25.6 μs, what is the minimum frame size?

Answer: Min Frame Size = 2 × Tprop × BW = 2 × 25.6 μs × 10 Mbps = 512 bits = 64 bytes.
Q3 — Conceptual
Why is CSMA/CA used in wireless LANs instead of CSMA/CD?

Answer: Wireless networks cannot detect collisions while transmitting because (1) the sender's own signal overpowers any collision signal, and (2) the hidden terminal problem means not all stations can hear each other. Therefore, collision avoidance using IFS and random backoff (contention window) is used instead of collision detection.
Q4 — Comparison
What is the vulnerable time in Pure ALOHA vs Slotted ALOHA? What does this mean for efficiency?

Answer: Pure ALOHA vulnerable time = 2 × Tframe (collision can occur Tf before or after current frame starts). Slotted ALOHA vulnerable time = 1 × Tframe. This halving of vulnerable time means Slotted ALOHA has exactly double the maximum throughput (36.8% vs 18.4%).
Q5 — Formula
What does the contention window size formula give in CSMA/CA? What happens as collisions increase?

Answer: Window Size = 2K − 1, where K is the attempt number. K=1 → 2 choices; K=2 → 4 choices; K=3 → 8 choices. As K increases, the window grows exponentially → stations are more spread out in time → lower collision probability. This is the same Binary Exponential Backoff principle as CSMA/CD.
Q6 — ALOHA Backoff
Stations on an ALOHA network are 600 km apart. List possible backoff times for K=1 and K=2.

Answer: Tp = 600×103 / 3×108 = 2 ms.
K=1: TB ∈ {0 ms, 2 ms}
K=2: TB ∈ {0 ms, 2 ms, 4 ms, 6 ms}
Q7 — Multiple Access Categories
Name and describe the three categories of multiple access protocols.

Answer:
① Random Access: Contention-based — any station transmits anytime, collision possible. Examples: ALOHA, CSMA/CD, CSMA/CA.
② Controlled Access: Stations take ordered turns (polling, token passing). No collision, but coordination required.
③ Channelization: Bandwidth divided among stations — FDMA (frequency), TDMA (time), CDMA (code).
Q8 — Persistence Methods
Compare 1-Persistent, Non-Persistent, and p-Persistent CSMA. Which is best and why?

Answer:
1-Persistent: Waits until idle, then transmits immediately. High collision risk when multiple stations waiting.
Non-Persistent: Waits random time before sensing again. Better efficiency but may leave channel idle unnecessarily.
p-Persistent: Transmits with probability p when idle; defers with (1−p). Best balance between collision reduction and channel utilization.
Q9 — Why Minimum Frame Size
Why must CSMA/CD frames have a minimum size? What determines this size?

Answer: The sender must still be transmitting when the collision signal arrives back. Worst case: station D starts transmitting just before A's signal reaches it, then the collision must travel all the way back. Min frame size = 2 × Tpropagation × Bandwidth. For 10 Mbps Ethernet with 25.6 μs propagation: 512 bits = 64 bytes.
Q10 — CSMA/CA Counter Freeze
In CSMA/CA, what happens to the countdown counter when the channel becomes busy?

Answer: The station freezes (pauses) its countdown counter. It does not reset. When the channel becomes idle again (after IFS), the station resumes counting from where it left off. This ensures stations that have already waited don't lose their progress — fairer distribution of access.
